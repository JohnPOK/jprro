Describe the Options for Automation, Device Management, and Network Management

Topic A

Automation, Network Management, and Device Management

There are many different tasks involved in managing Cisco networks. Some of them are quite repetitive in nature, which makes them good candidates for automation. Not only does this save you time, but it also helps reduce errors that can be introduced during implementation.

Automation

Automation is the process of reducing the need for human interaction to perform certain tasks. So, it should follow that network automation is the process of reducing the need for human interaction to configure, manage, deploy, test, and monitor physical and virtual network components. In today's computing environment, thousands of tasks are performed automatically on a regular basis, from downloading and applying software updates to automatic detection of security vulnerabilities. Automation is also a key ingredient in providing and managing cloud-based solutions.

  • Note: This is part of a larger movement generally known as DevOps, which is a set of practices that incorporates software development directly into IT operations.

How Automation Supports Network Management

When you use automation to augment network management, networks can perform the following tasks as a normal part of operation:

  • Discovering changes in topology
  • Updating and installing routes
  • Discovering routes that provide the best forwarding paths
  • Managing bandwidth
  • Updating software
  • Implementing security and compliance
  • Setting performance benchmarks
  • Performing root cause analysis of network issues

Not only does automation simplify network management tasks, but it also supports business or organization goals. The business benefits include:

  • Lower costs
  • Increased business continuity
  • Increased productivity of IT staff
  • Increased network control
  • Increased business agility, or the ability of a network to adapt to changes in the environment

Traditional Campus Device Management Practices

When you use traditional campus device management practices, you need to:

  • Manage each device separately.
  • Update each device separately.
  • Document each device separately.
  • Troubleshoot each device separately.

On a smaller LAN, this is manageable, but as you scale out, say, to a metro-wide or larger network, this approach becomes increasingly unwieldly and prone to errors and oversights.

Cisco Digital Network Architecture (DNA) Center

You can automate device management by implementing software such as Cisco DNA Center. DNA Center is a centralized management platform that provides a dashboard for every basic management task to simplify running your network. This product provides both controller management and analytics capabilities that enable you to quickly respond to changes and challenges. Cisco DNA Center also provides an open, extensible platform that supports external applications and systems to exchange data and intelligence and build on its native functionality.
